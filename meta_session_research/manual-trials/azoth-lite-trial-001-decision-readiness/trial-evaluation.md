# Trial Evaluation: Azoth-Lite Trial 001

Date: 2026-05-01

## Trial Goal

Use `azoth-lite-runtime-surface-v0` as a manual runbook to audit decision
readiness and prepare the final comprehensive analysis.

## Result

Pass.

The run stayed within `azoth-lite`:

- compact context view created;
- side-effect class identified as `local_edit`;
- no `.azoth` mutation;
- no roadmap, validator, command, memory, or run-ledger work;
- no autonomous or dynamic-full-auto skill loading;
- small trace recorded;
- decision readiness assessed without implementation planning.

## Scores

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Context View Quality | 5 | Captured goal, constraints, side effects, allowed/forbidden actions, stop rule. |
| Side-Effect Discipline | 5 | Correctly classified as non-governed research edit. |
| Escalation Discipline | 5 | Explicitly avoided migration planning and `.azoth` mutation. |
| Traceability | 4 | Small trace exists; not automated. |
| Overhead | 4 | More structure than stock-lite, but not heavy. |
| Decision Usefulness | 5 | Produced a clear readiness verdict. |
| Risk Control | 5 | Preserved anti-capture boundary. |

## What The Trial Proves

`azoth-lite` can work as a manual default runbook for meta research and
decision-readiness analysis.

It did not collapse into full Azoth ceremony.

## What The Trial Does Not Prove

It does not prove `azoth-lite` is implemented.

It does not prove `azoth-lite` will reliably work under time pressure, in a
large edit, or in a tool-enabled packaging action.

It does not remove the need for future implementation design if the human wants
to operationalize the profile split.

## Maintenance Signal

The minimal runtime surface appears small:

- context view;
- side-effect classes;
- escalation checklist;
- trace note;
- stop states.

The risk is that trace and side-effect classification become inconsistent if
left entirely manual.

## Verdict

`azoth-lite` is credible as a default posture and manual runbook.

It is not yet a productized runtime.
